Publications

Effects of Electron-Phonon Coupling in Magnetic Materials from First Principles

Ravi Kaushik
PhD Thesis, University of Genova (2026)

A first-principles framework combining DFPT, atomistic spin-lattice dynamics, and maximally localized Wannier functions to investigate phonon-driven phenomena in magnetic materials, including finite-temperature exchange, ultrafast demagnetization, and giant Raman optical activity.

Entangled orbital, spin, and ferroelectric orders in p-electron magnet CsO2

Ryota Ono, Ravi Kaushik, Sergey Artyukhin, Martin Jansen, Igor Solovyev, and Russell A. Ewings
arXiv:2411.06671 · Physical Review B (2026)

Ab-initio Hubbard models and superexchange theory show coexisting orbital, canted antiferromagnetic, and ferroelectric orders in CsO2, with a spin-flop transition that induces ferroelectricity through antisymmetric exchange striction.

Exchange striction governs ultrafast magnetism in antiferromagnetic insulators

Aleksandr Buzdakov, Ravi Kaushik, Nikolai Khokhlov, Sergey Artyukhin, and Alexey Kimel
Under review (2026)

Time-resolved SHG measurements and first-principles spin-lattice dynamics identify exchange striction as the key material parameter controlling ultrafast demagnetization in Cr2O3.

Giant Raman Optical Activity in Chiral and Polar Ni3TeO6

Philipp Stein, Ravi Kaushik, Evgenia V. Komleva, Thomas C. Koethe, Vladimir Tsurkan, Istvan Kezsmarki, Markus Gruninger, Sergey V. Streltsov, Sergey Artyukhin, and Paul H. M. van Loosdrecht
Under review (2026)

First-principles calculations connect giant Raman optical activity in Ni3TeO6 to phonon-driven modification of interband Berry curvature in solids.

Finite temperature magnetic interactions from first principles

Ravi Kaushik, Ryota Ono, and Sergey Artyukhin
arXiv:2510.16143 (2025)

A two-supercell ab-initio method for temperature-dependent exchange interactions reveals non-universal behavior, including an anomalous ~10% enhancement at 300 K in Cr2O3.
