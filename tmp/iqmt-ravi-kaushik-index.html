Publications

Effects of Electron-Phonon Coupling in Magnetic Materials from First Principles

Ravi Kaushik
PhD Thesis, University of Genova (2026)

Combines DFPT, atomistic spin-lattice dynamics, and maximally localized Wannier functions into a unified first-principles framework to investigate phonon-driven phenomena in magnetic materials: finite-temperature exchange, ultrafast demagnetization in antiferromagnetic insulators, and giant Raman optical activity via Berry curvature modification by phonons.

Entangled orbital, spin, and ferroelectric orders in p-electron magnet CsO2

Ryota Ono, Ravi Kaushik, Sergey Artyukhin, Martin Jansen, Igor Solovyev, and Russell A. Ewings
arXiv:2411.06671 · Physical Review B (2026)

Ab-initio Hubbard models and superexchange theory reveal that CsO2, a p-electron magnet, exhibits coexisting orbital, canted antiferromagnetic, and ferroelectric orders. A spin-flop transition induces ferroelectricity via an antisymmetric exchange striction mechanism, making CsO2 a rare multiferroic beyond the d/f-electron paradigm.

Exchange striction governs ultrafast magnetism in antiferromagnetic insulators

Aleksandr Buzdakov, Ravi Kaushik, Nikolai Khokhlov, Sergey Artyukhin, and Alexey Kimel
Under Review (2026)

Time-resolved SHG measurements show Cr2O3 demagnetizes in ~2 ps — two orders of magnitude faster than FeBO3. First-principles spin-lattice dynamics identifies exchange striction (displacement derivative of magnetic exchange) as the controlling material parameter, computable entirely from ab initio inputs.

Giant Raman Optical Activity in Chiral and Polar Ni3TeO6

Philipp Stein, Ravi Kaushik, Evgenia V. Komleva, Thomas C. Koethe, Vladimir Tsurkan, István Kézsmárki, Markus Grüninger, Sergey V. Streltsov, Sergey Artyukhin, and Paul H. M. van Loosdrecht
Under Review (2026)

Ni3TeO6 exhibits a 100× giant Raman optical activity compared to typical solids. First-principles calculations establish dynamic modification of interband Berry curvature through phonon excitation as the origin of ROA in solids. Chiral phonons are confirmed but do not contribute to ROA for light along the c-axis.

Finite temperature magnetic interactions from first principles

Ravi Kaushik, Ryota Ono, and Sergey Artyukhin
arXiv:2510.16143 (2025)

Two-supercell ab-initio method for temperature-dependent exchange interactions reveals non-universal behavior: NiO exchange decreases with temperature (180° bonds), while Cr2O3 shows an anomalous ~10% enhancement at 300 K driven by bond-angle changes and band-gap evolution.
